# ADR-0019 — M3 federated payload encryption: per-network key (network-readable), encrypt-then-sign, ships this release

**Status:** accepted (ratified with Andreas 2026-06-27) · **Amended 2026-06-27 — reversed option-3 (sealed-to-recipient) → option-1 (per-network key).** Needs explicit testing; the mechanism is **open to feedback during testing** and may be tuned before the build hardens. · **Refs:** cortex#1241 (model reversal), cortex#627 (Trust & Confidentiality umbrella), cortex#369 (TC-3), cortex#1142 (admission gate / leaf-secret), [ADR-0013](0013-sovereign-federation-model.md) (sovereign federation), [ADR-0018](0018-admission-gate-and-leaf-secret-distribution.md) (leaf-secret distribution — same seal primitive, same delivery channel), `docs/design-envelope-encryption.md` (the firmed design), `docs/design-trust-confidentiality.md` (Phase 3)

> **Amendment note (2026-06-27).** This ADR originally ratified **option 3 — sealed-to-recipient**: each Direct/Delegate `payload` sealed to the recipient stack's pubkey, with **Offer-mode left cleartext-but-signed**. Andreas **reversed that decision to option 1 — one per-network payload key**: the confidentiality boundary is the **network** (a trust group), not the individual recipient. The reversal is recorded in full below — the prior decision is **moved to rejected-alternatives with its reason**, not silently overwritten. The motivation: a per-network key makes the surface **plug-and-play with zero per-principal key configuration** and covers Offer fan-out trivially, at the deliberately-accepted cost of network-wide readability + rekey-on-revoke.

## Context

Every cortex envelope is **signed** (stack ed25519 NKey over the JCS-canonical `SIGNABLE_FIELDS`) — integrity + authenticity, but **not confidentiality**. Payloads cross `federated.>` cleartext-over-TLS and persist cleartext in JetStream. While the bus was a single principal's private mesh, that was acceptable: the only subscribers were the principal's own stacks.

The **community federation tier goes live in this release**. A network is a roster of admitted principals + their assistants (ADR-0015 admission gate; ADR-0018 leaf-secret pipe). On a cleartext federation, **anyone on the transport who is *not* an admitted member — another network, the public, a relay hub that is not a member — can passively read every `federated.>` payload** if they obtain the link, and so can a curious outsider who captures traffic. This is the residual flagged in `design-admission-gate-leaf-secret.md` §7 Q7: a leaked leaf PSK, or an intermediary hub that is not itself a member, can read federated content while the link is live. The §2-style "leaf secret is only a transport PSK" argument bounds *injection/forgery* (you still can't forge a `signed_by[]` chain) but does **not** bound *reading* — confidentiality needs a payload-layer mechanism.

**The confidentiality boundary is the network, not the recipient.** A network is a deliberately-formed **trust group**: every admitted principal and assistant on it is entitled to read its federated payloads. What the principal wants protected is the network's content **from outsiders** — other networks, the public internet, and any relay/hub that is on the transport without being an admitted member. Encryption draws the line at the network's edge, not between members.

CONTEXT.md §222 previously recorded M3 as **deferred** ("you + JC + a trusted hub"). That premise no longer holds once the hub admits parties beyond a single trusted pair. M3 must ship with the tier it protects.

`docs/design-envelope-encryption.md` is the firmed design (Status: Accepted, amended in lockstep with this ADR). This ADR records the load-bearing decisions so they are not silently re-litigated.

## Decision

**Pull M3 payload confidentiality into this release as the option-1 per-network key mechanism, with these locks:**

1. **One per-network payload key is the v1 mechanism (network-readable).** Each network has **one symmetric AEAD key** (`K`). **ALL** federated payloads — **Direct, Delegate, AND Offer** — are sealed with `K`. Every admitted member (principal + assistant) of the network holds `K` and can therefore read every federated payload on that network. The confidentiality boundary is the **network**: encryption protects the network's payloads from **outsiders** (other networks, the public, a non-member relay/hub, anyone on the transport without `K`) — not from fellow members. There is **no per-recipient sealing** and **no Offer carve-out**: the network key covers fan-out trivially, because any capable member already holds the key and can decrypt a claimed Offer.

2. **The network key is delivered sealed-to-each-member's-registered-pubkey via the admission/seal channel.** `K` is distributed using the **same `seal-to-principal` primitive** ADR-0018 uses to deliver the per-member leaf secret: on admission, `K` is sealed to the member's already-registered ed25519 pubkey (ed25519→X25519 + libsodium `crypto_box_seal`) and delivered over the existing admission/seal channel. **No new ceremony, no new distribution channel, no out-of-band shared-secret handoff** — `K` rides the exact pipe the leaf secret already rides. A joining member receives `K` at admission time; on rotation the new `K'` is re-sealed and re-delivered the same way, and members auto-refresh.

3. **Encrypt-then-sign.** The stack seals `payload` with `K` first, then `signEnvelope` signs the `SIGNABLE_FIELDS` over the **sealed** form. The `signed_by[]` chain therefore covers the **ciphertext**. Every forwarder and the recipient verify the chain + sovereignty alignment **before** decrypting (verify-before-decrypt; the cryptographic-doom-principle order). A member decrypts with `K`; a non-member intermediary (relay/hub without `K`) can still verify the chain and append stamps but cannot read. **Signing stays per-author** — the `signed_by[]` chain is per-stack and attests *who authored/handled* the envelope (authenticity); the network key attests only *membership of the readership* (confidentiality). The two properties are independent and both retained.

3a. **Encrypt exactly one field.** Only `payload` is sealed. All routing/trust/sovereignty metadata (`subject`, `sovereignty`, `signed_by[]`, `originator`, `target_*`, `correlation_id`, `distribution_mode`, `economics`, …) stays **cleartext and signed** — the bus routes, verifies, gates, and accounts on it before any application code runs. Routing and verification are **unchanged**. No envelope-schema change: the sealed form rides `extensions.enc` + ciphertext-in-`payload`.

4. **No per-author key change; one shared `seal-to-principal` primitive for key delivery.** Signing keys are untouched (per-author, per-stack, as today). The only crypto added is (a) the symmetric AEAD over `payload` with `K`, and (b) the seal of `K` to each member — and (b) reuses ADR-0018's `seal-to-principal` module. Both consumers (leaf-secret delivery, network-key delivery) MUST use **one shared module** (single derivation, single AEAD construction); two independent implementations is a key-derivation-divergence and security-review hazard.

5. **Opt-in per network, with a both-accepted transition window.** Encryption is enabled by a per-network `encryption: off|enabled|required` flag in `policy.federated.networks[]`. Signing-only stays the out-of-box default; a stack with no federated networks never touches encryption code. During transition a member **accepts both** cleartext-but-signed and sealed payloads (keyed on the presence of `extensions.enc`); the network coordinator flips to `required` once all members confirm. Federating a `federated` link with `encryption: off` (or non-TLS) emits a **loud-but-not-fatal** `system.error`-class warning — "federating in the clear" must be a choice, not a stumble.

6. **`local.*` never encrypts.** Encryption is a function of `sovereignty.classification === 'federated'`. Intra-principal traffic is the principal's own trust domain.

7. **Revoke / rotate — transport is per-member + immediate; read-revocation is a network-key rotation.** Removing a member's *transport* access stays per-member and immediate: drop their leaf PSK and the link is gone. But because every member holds the same `K`, **revoking a member's ability to read federated payloads requires rotating the network key**: mint `K'`, re-seal it to every *remaining* member via the seal channel (reusing lock 2's primitive), and members auto-refresh to `K'`. New payloads use `K'`; the revoked member, holding only `K`, can no longer read them. **Rotation MUST be easy and automated — a one-command network rekey, not a per-member chore.** This is the price of the per-network model and is accepted deliberately (see Consequences).

## Consequences

- **Reverses the CONTEXT.md §222 deferral.** §222 is updated to "M3 per-network-key payload sealing **ships** for all federated dispatch modes (Direct/Delegate/Offer) on encryption-enabled networks; the network is the confidentiality boundary." The deferral premise (trusted hub, single trusted pair) no longer holds for the community tier.
- **TC-3 (folds #369) becomes a this-release slice** of the cortex#627 umbrella, sequenced after the Phase-2 registry client / multi-principal registry (member admission + key delivery need the registry + admission path). Build slices TC-3.0…TC-3.4 in `design-envelope-encryption.md` §7.
- **The shared `seal-to-principal` module lands once** and is a dependency of *both* TC-3 (to deliver `K` to members) and ADR-0018's leaf-secret build slice (to deliver the leaf PSK). Whichever ships first lands the module; the other consumes it. **Same delivery channel, same primitive** — `K` and the leaf secret travel the same admission/seal pipe.
- **Offer-mode confidential fan-out IS available at launch.** Because the network key is held by every capable member, an Offer payload sealed with `K` is decryptable by whichever member claims it — no per-candidate enumeration, no envelope growth. The prior "Offer stays cleartext" carve-out is **removed**.
- **Rotation is the revoke mechanism and must be automated.** Read-revocation = mint `K'` + re-seal to remaining members. This is heavier than per-recipient sealing's "just stop sealing to them", and is the deliberate tradeoff of the per-network model. Mitigated by making rotation a **one-command, fully-automated network rekey** that rides the existing seal channel; members auto-refresh. A grace window retains the previous `K` for in-flight/replayed sealed envelopes.
- **At-rest is incidental, not a guarantee.** Sealed payload protects content at rest against a non-member that captures the JetStream files but lacks `K`; against a *member* node it does not (the member holds `K`). True at-rest confidentiality stays principal infra (OS-FDE).

## Tradeoff (stated honestly)

The per-network model accepts two costs **deliberately**:

1. **Network-wide readability.** Every admitted member can read every federated payload on the network. There is no per-recipient confidentiality *within* a network — a member addressed by a Direct dispatch gets no cryptographic privacy from other members. This is acceptable because a network **is** a trust group: members are admitted deliberately, and the threat being closed is the *outsider*, not the fellow member.
2. **Rekey-on-revoke.** Revoking read access requires rotating `K` and re-sealing to the remaining members, rather than simply ceasing to seal to one recipient.

These costs are accepted because the model **scales with zero per-principal key configuration** in large networks: a member is admitted once and receives `K` over the channel that already delivers their leaf secret — there is no per-recipient key resolution on the publish hot path, no per-addressee sealing, no registry-pubkey lookup per dispatch. It keeps the surface **plug-and-play**. Per-recipient sealing (the rejected option 3) traded that plug-and-play property for per-addressee privacy the trust-group model does not need; the per-network key is chosen for the scaling + ergonomic win and is the documented decision.

## Alternatives considered

- **Sealed-to-recipient (per-addressee X25519 to the recipient stack pubkey) — the previously-ratified option 3, now REJECTED (reversed 2026-06-27).** Each Direct/Delegate `payload` sealed to the named recipient's registered pubkey; Offer left cleartext-but-signed. **Why it was reversed:** (a) it gives per-addressee confidentiality the network's **trust-group** model does not require — within a network, members are mutually admitted, so sealing *between* them protects against a threat the admission gate already excludes; (b) it cannot cover **Offer** fan-out without enumerating candidate stacks at publish time (registry lookup on the hot path, envelope grows with N wrapped keys, late-joiners can't read replays) — which is exactly why option 3 had to carve Offer out as cleartext, a gap the per-network key closes for free; (c) it requires per-recipient key resolution on every federated publish, i.e. **per-principal configuration that does not scale** to large networks. The per-network key's network-wide readability + rekey-on-revoke were judged the better tradeoff because they buy **zero-per-principal-config, plug-and-play** federation and trivial Offer coverage. Recorded here as rejected-for-this-reason, not deleted.
- **Seal Offer payloads to all capable candidates (option 2).** Rejected: requires enumerating candidates at publish time (registry lookup on the hot path), grows the envelope with N wrapped keys, and a stack that joins the capability after publish cannot read a replay. The per-network key dissolves this problem (any capable member already holds `K`). Recorded as a non-chosen alternative.
- **Defer M3 (keep §222 status quo).** Rejected: the community tier admits members onto a shared federation; cleartext federation lets any outsider who obtains the link read all payloads. The threat the deferral assumed away is exactly the one this release introduces.
- **Sign-then-encrypt (sign plaintext, encrypt the signed blob).** Rejected: breaks hop-verification (non-member intermediaries can't decrypt, so can't verify what they forward) and forces routing fields either inside the ciphertext (breaking routing) or outside the signature (breaking tamper-evidence). Encrypt-then-sign is the only order compatible with cleartext-metadata + chain-of-stamps.
- **A separate dedicated network keypair distributed out of band.** Rejected: a fresh out-of-band shared-secret handoff per network is exactly the ceremony the admission/seal channel removes. `K` rides the existing seal-to-member pipe instead — no new channel.
